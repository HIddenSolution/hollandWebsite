Holland Construction

is an agricultural, residential, and commercial construction company that specializes in dirt work and hauling. Our dirt work expertise covers driveways and parking areas, stock tank builds and reconstructions, demolition, residential and commercial pad building, and agricultural, commercial, or residential land clearing. About

Holland Construction Inc is a family owned and operated company.

Holland Construction Inc. is owned and operated by Sheldon and Marsha Scott. Sheldon grew up on a farm in central texas. He started operating equipment at the age of 7 years old. At 15 he found his love of dozers and dirt and started learning that to be a true steward of the land is to help the land be as productive as possible while also allowing a habitat for wildlife to flourish.


At 20 years of age he was hired on with an oil field and commercial dirt work contractor and soon was a crew foreman learning and adopting ways to move the earth with efficiency and quality in mind. At 24 he and his Father in law started building a dirt work division of then ( Holland Construction and Remodeling LLC) with the purchase of their first dump truck. Since, we have grown our fleet and that has allowed us to offer a variety of needs to our customers. Sheldon and Marsha’s main mission is to provide a great product and experience for our clients.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you’ve got a question, we’ve got an answer.

How far do you haul and conduct business? We will haul as far as needed. The price up to 50 miles is calculated by the loaded mile. Mileage exceeding 50 loaded miles is subject to a return-home fee. We do try to use the closest supplier near you to ensure the best price possible.
What if any accidents occur during a job? Holland Construction Inc is properly insured to handle all emergencies and/or accidents that may occur. We take pride in practicing appropriate safety precautions to prevent any accidents from happening.
Do I need a permit to do my dirt work? If you are inside city limits you may need to check your city's regulations. If you need work on or next to a state highway you will need to contact your nearest TX DOT.
